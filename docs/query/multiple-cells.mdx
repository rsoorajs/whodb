# Scratchpad Pages And Cells

Scratchpad organizes SQL into pages and cells.

- **Pages** are tabs across the top of Scratchpad.
- **Cells** are individual SQL editors stacked inside a page.

![Scratchpad Multiple Cells](/images/31-scratchpad-multiple-pages.png)

## Pages

Use pages to separate workflows, investigations, or database tasks.

<Steps>
<Step title="Create A Page">
Click the plus tab in the Scratchpad page bar.
</Step>
<Step title="Switch Pages">
Click a page tab.
</Step>
<Step title="Rename A Page">
Double-click the page name and type the new name.
</Step>
<Step title="Delete A Page">
Click the close button on the page tab and confirm deletion.
</Step>
</Steps>

## Cells

Use cells to keep related queries visible in one page.

<Steps>
<Step title="Add A Cell">
Hover over an existing cell and click the add-cell control.
</Step>
<Step title="Run A Cell">
Use the blue run button or press `Cmd/Ctrl + Enter` while focused in the cell.
</Step>
<Step title="Clear A Cell">
Use the clear-cell control or press `Cmd/Ctrl + U`.
</Step>
<Step title="Delete A Cell">
Use the delete-cell control. A page must keep at least one cell.
</Step>
</Steps>

![Multiple Cells Results](/images/48-scratchpad-multiple-cells-results.png)

## Cell Options Menu

The cell options menu currently contains:

- **Clear**
- **Copy Code**
- **Query History**

![Cell Options Menu](/images/32-scratchpad-cell-options-menu.png)

Use the visible page tabs and cell controls for Scratchpad organization.
